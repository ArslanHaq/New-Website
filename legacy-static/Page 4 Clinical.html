First-in-Human Oncology

Phase 1a clinical results: PRX-474

Completed dose-escalation trial in 17 patients with advanced solid tumors who had failed all prior standard-of-care therapies.

Trial Overview

Study Design

3+3 dose-escalation trial conducted 2019–2021. ClinicalTrials.gov: NCT04811898.

Patients Enrolled

17 cancer patients evaluated for response. Diagnoses included pancreatic, glioblastoma, CRC, HCC, breast, ovarian, gastric, and peritoneal mesothelioma.

Dose Range

0.5 to 5 mg/kg tested. All patients had failed previous standard-of-care therapies.

Key Results

Safety & efficacy signals

Safety (Primary Outcome)

0grade 3–4 toxicities

Well tolerated across all dose levels (0.5–5 mg/kg). MTD was not reached.

5 mg/kg

Recommended dose for future trials.

Efficacy (Secondary Outcome)

56%disease control rate

Stable disease in 50% of patients, partial response in 6.25% (CRC). Total SD + PR: 56.25%.

44+months durable response

Metastatic CRC patient alive with excellent performance status at 44+ months.

"The excellent safety profile, the promising bio-modulatory and the anti-tumor activity strongly support translation into a Phase 1b/Phase 2a trial."

Pharmacodynamics

Mechanism confirmed

The trial demonstrated concentration-dependent downregulation of miR-221 and upregulation of its canonical targets — confirming the LockMiR™ mechanism of action in patients.

Unexpected Clinical Benefit: CIPN

During the Phase 1a trial, symptomatic clinical benefit was observed in patients affected by chemotherapy-induced peripheral neuropathy (CIPN) — a debilitating side effect with no current FDA-approved treatment. Among 13 patients who experienced CIPN, improvement was documented on the PGC neurological assessment scale. This unexpected finding opens a potential additional therapeutic application for LockMiR™ technology.

What's Next

Path to Phase 1b/2a

The excellent safety profile and promising anti-tumor activity support advancement into a confirmatory Phase 1a/b dose optimization and expansion trial — as a single agent and/or combination — in advanced HCC and CRC.
